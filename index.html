Test format

How the test works

The standard English proficiency test administered across all Meghe Group of Schools. Four sections, equal weighting, taken in a fixed order.

1. Listening
→
2. Reading
→
3. Spoken English
→
4. Extempo speech
SectionFormatTimeScoring basis
Listening Audio passage, 5 MCQs 5 min 20 marks per question, 100% total
Reading Passage on screen, 5 MCQs 6 min 20 marks per question, 100% total
Spoken English Read given paragraph aloud 5 min Word accuracy, speed, fluency, pronunciation, voice modulation
Extempo Speech Speak on a chosen topic (1 min prep + up to 3 min) 4 min Fluency, vocabulary range, topic relevance, pronunciation, voice modulation
Scoring key

Standard Grade Rubrics

Overall score = ( Listening + Reading + Spoken English + Extempo Speech ) ÷ 4

ScoreGradeMeaning
90% and above Excellent Strong English proficiency
80 – 89% Good Good communication, minor improvement needed
70 – 79% Average / Needs Improvement Support required in fluency, accuracy or confidence
60 – 69% Needs Support Requires structured language training
Below 60% Requires Intensive Support Requires intensive training and close monitoring

Roadmap

Way Forward

Building on the TEP results, here's how this work extends from assessing teachers to supporting classrooms directly.

01

Classroom Observation App

A classroom observation app has already been developed, with a built-in recording option to support structured observation and review.

Open the Classroom Observation App →

02

Live classroom audio — Grades 1 to 3

3 webcams per school are planned, with audio live-streamed in rotation across classrooms, to extend observation coverage for Grades 1 to 3.

03

Student skill assessment — Grades 1 to 5

For Grades 1 to 5, an app-based assessment of Listening, Reading and Speaking skills is planned, following a similar model to this TEP portal. Reading passages will be drawn from Fitzroy Readers, set one level below each student's actual grade.
